Naoko Takeuchi

Naoko Takeuchi is a Japanese manga artist. She has won several awards for her work and later married Yoshihiro Togashi, the author of YuYu Hakusho and Hunter x Hunter.

    Early Life:

  • Born March 15, 1967 in Kofu, Yamanashi, Japan
  • Attended Kofu Ichi High School where inspirations of her work began
  • At 19, graduated from Kyoritsu College of Pkarmacy with a degree in chemistry
  • Submitted first work, Love Call, to Kodansha

    • 1986 - 1997:

  • For "Love Call", she won Nakayoshi's New Artist award which debuted in the Nakayoshi Deluxe September 1986 issue
  • Takeuchi's first serial comic, Maria was published in Nakayoshi from early to mid-1990
  • Worked on the ice-skating series The Cherry Project from late 1990 to 1991
  • Worked on a manga about outerspace and girl fighters, which became Codename: Sailor V while working on The Cherry Project
  • In December 1991, Nakayoshi serialized Sailor Moon
  • Won the 17th Kodansha Manga Award for "Sailor Moon" in 1993
  • Takeuchi started her own studio called 'Princess Naoko Planning' (PNP) to manage Sailor Moon

    • 1998 - 2009

  • Togashi and Takeuchi married in 1999 and had two children: a son in 2001 and a daughter in 2009
  • In 1999, Kodansha's rights to Sailor Moon expired so Takeuchi worked on the reprints of "Sailor Moon" and "Sailor V"
  • In 2003, Takeuchi was involved in producing the TV series Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon, based on Sailor Moon

    • 2010 - 2019

  • A new "Sailor Moon" anime adaptation was in development in 2012 and debuted in 2014
  • By June 2016, the series ended with three seasons

    • 2020 - present

  • In 2021, Takeuchi chiefly supervised "Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon Eternal The Movie" anime film which acted as the fourth season to the series
  • In 2022, Takeuchi chiefly supervised the sequel to the movie "Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon Cosmo The Movie", which acted as the fifth and final season of the series
